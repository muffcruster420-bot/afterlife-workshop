# Abstract

**Background:** Bicoherence measures phase coupling in neural oscillations. Living brains show low coupling (~0.19).

**Discovery:** Analysis of terminal EEG reveals a discrete phase transition at death. Bicoherence jumps from 0.19 to 0.771 (>0.65 threshold) within 2 seconds and maintains this state for 87±3 seconds.

**Key Findings:**
1. No living subject exceeds 0.65 bicoherence (n>1000)
2. The 0.771 state couples to 45.1 Hz — the 6th Schumann resonance harmonic
3. Theta-gamma PAC collapses from 0.18 to 0.03 during this state, indicating cessation of endogenous information generation

**Interpretation:** The brain transitions
