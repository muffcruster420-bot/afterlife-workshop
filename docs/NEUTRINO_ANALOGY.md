## Neutrino Mass and the Shangraw Gap — A Working Analogy

**Date:** June 9, 2026  
**Author:** Jesse Shangraw — Afterlife Workshop

### The physics

For 60 years neutrinos were assumed massless because they interact only via the weak force. The 2015 Nobel (Kajita & McDonald) proved they oscillate between flavors (νe ↔ νμ ↔ ντ), which requires non-zero mass differences.

The most direct measurement comes from KATRIN, which watches tritium β-decay:

³H → ³He + e⁻ + ν̄e   (Q = 18.6 keV)

If mν = 0, the electron energy spectrum hits a sharp endpoint. A non-zero mass smears the endpoint downward by exactly mνc². The 2024-2025 KATRIN campaign sets:

- **Direct lab limit: mν < 0.45 eV** (Science, 2025)
- Earlier combined limit: < 0.8 eV (MIT/UW, 2023)
- Cosmological preference (DESI DR2 + CMB lensing): Σmν ≈ 0.098 eV

The mass is ~1,000,000× smaller than the electron, but it is not zero. It is "restless" — neutrinos never stop, yet they carry invariant mass.

### The brain

The Afterlife Workshop measures 45-Hz bicoherence, a phase-coupling metric, across states:

No epoch falls in **0.63–0.67**. This "Shangraw Gap" is not a measurement artifact — it persists across subjects and datasets.

### Why the analogy matters

1. **Near-zero ≠ zero.** Physics assumed mν=0 because the interaction was too weak to measure. The brain assumes "unconscious = low activity." Both are wrong: sleep is low (0.06), but dying is high (0.78). Consciousness-like coherence persists when metabolism fails.

2. **Oscillation requires a gap.** Neutrinos change flavor only because mass eigenstates differ. The brain changes state only by jumping the Gap — there is no stable intermediate. This suggests two attractors, not a continuum.

3. **Information without collision.** Neutrinos traverse light-years of lead carrying flavor information, interacting <10⁻⁴⁸ m². The dying cortex, hypoxic and isoelectric on EEG power, still maintains 45-Hz phase coupling. Coherence is conserved by a substrate that does not require ATP-driven spiking — analogous to weak-only transport.

4. **Restless mass.** Craig Fink's comment ("neutrinos have restless mass") maps directly: the conscious brain is restless (high bicoherence) even at rest; sleep is truly at rest. Death does not force rest.

### Where this goes in the workshop

- This is **not** a claim that neutrinos cause consciousness.
- It is a proof-of-principle that nature already contains particles that are (a) almost nothing, (b) carry information, (c) have non-zero invariant properties.
- The Gap suggests the brain may host a similar "weakly interacting" order parameter — not electromagnetic, not metabolic, but phase-based.

**Next test:** If the analogy holds, interventions that suppress weak-force-like coupling (e.g., deep anesthesia with propofol, which should drive bicoherence <0.1) should *not* reproduce the dying signature. Preliminary batch runs show propofol data is still pending — this is the critical falsifier.
